# Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

## What is it?
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of online financial 
crime where fraudsters trick victims into sending money or revealing 
their UPI PIN through deceptive methods and then rob all the money available online. 

## How it typically happens
1. The fraudster contacts the victim via phone or WhatsApp or personal messages, 
   pretending to be a bank official or known contact.
2. They claim the victim has won a prize or needs to verify 
   their account urgently or sometime pretend to be a distant relative and asks for help.
3. They send a "collect request" on the victim's UPI app, 
   which looks like a payment incoming — but actually 
   withdraws money when approved.
4. The victim, confused, enters their UPI PIN thinking 
   they are receiving money.
5. Money is instantly debited from their account.

## Who is targeted?
College students, elderly people, and first-time UPI users 
are most commonly targeted due to limited awareness.

## Consequences
- Immediate financial loss with little chance of recovery
- Emotional distress and loss of trust in digital payments
- In some cases, victims take out loans to cover losses

## Source
Based on common fraud patterns reported by the National 
Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in)
